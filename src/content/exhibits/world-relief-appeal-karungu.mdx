World Relief, the humanitarian arm of the National Association of Evangelicals, designed this 2025 direct mail package to support its refugee resettlement work. The appeal tells the story of Karungu, a refugee from the Democratic Republic of the Congo who spent 16 years in displacement before being welcomed to the United States through World Relief's Refugee Resettlement program. The headline — "Your Compassion Opens Doors That Fear Tried to Close" — anchors the package in a specific human journey. The multi-piece package includes a letter signed by the president, a full-color insert with impact amounts ($500 to $5,000), a response device, and a return envelope.

The package exemplifies modern direct mail at its best: professional design that reflects the organization's brand, a compelling personal narrative, and a response device with specific gift amounts tied to concrete outcomes — "$500 gives 20 families access to clean water," "$2,500 provides 12 families in the midst of crisis." The inclusion of a QR code alongside traditional mail-back options acknowledges that today's direct mail donors may prefer to complete their gift online. World Relief's appeal demonstrates that direct mail continues to evolve while retaining its core strength: storytelling that makes donors feel personally connected to the people they help.
