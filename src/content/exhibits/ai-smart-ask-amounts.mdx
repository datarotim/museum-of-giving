By 2023, multiple fundraising platforms had deployed machine learning models to dynamically set suggested donation amounts for each visitor. Rather than showing every donor the same $25 / $50 / $100 options, the form analyzed available signals — past giving history, wealth indicators, browsing behavior, device type, referring source — and presented a personalized ask string optimized to maximize the expected gift. A lapsed donor might see a lower re-engagement amount; a major gift prospect might see amounts starting at $500.

This represented the arrival of true personalization in the donation experience — the fundraising equivalent of the product recommendations that had transformed e-commerce years earlier. Early results showed that AI-optimized ask strings could increase average gift size by 15-30% compared to static amounts. The technology also raised important questions about the ethics of algorithmic persuasion in philanthropy: when a machine learns the optimal amount to ask for, who benefits — the cause, the donor, or the platform? These questions will define the next era of fundraising technology, as AI moves from optimizing the ask to potentially generating the entire appeal.
