The fundraising letter is the heart of any direct mail package. Typically two to four pages long, printed on the organization's letterhead, and signed by a real person, the letter follows a format refined over decades of testing: open with a story or urgent situation, introduce a specific person affected, explain what the organization is doing, make a clear and specific ask, and close with a postscript that restates the urgency. The best fundraising letters read like personal correspondence — conversational, emotional, and direct.

Direct mail copywriters discovered a counterintuitive truth: longer letters almost always outperformed shorter ones. A four-page letter beat a one-page letter. Underlining and bold text increased response. A P.S. at the bottom was read more often than the body copy. These findings, validated through millions of pieces mailed and measured, created a body of knowledge about persuasive writing that predated and anticipated digital marketing by decades. The fundraising letter was the original content marketing — a format where storytelling, data, emotion, and a clear call to action combined to move people from awareness to action in the space of a few pages.
