By 2003, the ASPCA was using Convio (later acquired by Blackbaud) to power its online donation forms — one of the early examples of Software-as-a-Service in the nonprofit sector. Convio provided a hosted, configurable donation platform that organizations could deploy without building custom technology. The forms included features like recurring gift options, tribute giving, employer matching prompts, and email opt-ins.

Convio's arrival marked a critical maturation of the nonprofit technology landscape. Before SaaS platforms, building an online donation capability required custom web development — expensive, slow, and beyond the reach of most organizations. Convio (alongside competitors like Kintera and Blackbaud) packaged best practices into a product: optimized form layouts, secure payment processing, CRM integration, and automated donor communications. This standardization had a leveling effect. A regional animal shelter could now offer a donation experience comparable to a billion-dollar national charity. The SaaS model for nonprofit fundraising, born in this era, would eventually become the dominant paradigm.
