DonorsChoose, founded by Bronx teacher Charles Best in 2000, hit its stride by the mid-2000s with a model that felt genuinely new: public school teachers posted specific classroom project requests, complete with itemized budgets, and donors chose which projects to fund. When a project was fully funded, DonorsChoose purchased the materials and shipped them directly to the classroom. Donors received photos and thank-you letters from students.

The platform solved multiple problems simultaneously. It gave donors unprecedented transparency — you knew exactly what your money bought. It gave teachers agency to identify their own needs rather than waiting for institutional budgets. And it made giving tangibly satisfying: you could watch a progress bar fill up and know that 25 students would get new books because of your $50. DonorsChoose has since channeled over $1 billion to classrooms and proved that specificity and transparency are among the most powerful tools in fundraising. People give more when they can see precisely where the money goes.
