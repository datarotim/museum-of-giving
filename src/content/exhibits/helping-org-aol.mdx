Launched in 1999 by the AOL Foundation, Helping.org was one of the first web platforms to aggregate charitable organizations into a searchable directory where visitors could find and donate to any nonprofit. The site let users search by cause, location, or organization name, then make a donation through a unified payment process. It was, in essence, the first online giving marketplace.

The concept was ahead of its time. Helping.org anticipated the platform model that would later be realized by Network for Good, JustGiving, GoFundMe, and Every.org — the idea that a single trusted intermediary could reduce the friction of giving by handling payment processing, tax receipts, and charity verification. The platform addressed a real problem: in 1999, the vast majority of nonprofits had no online donation capability whatsoever. By providing a shared infrastructure, Helping.org made online giving accessible to thousands of organizations that couldn't have built it themselves.
