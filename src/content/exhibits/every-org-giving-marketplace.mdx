Every.org launched as a modern giving marketplace — a single platform where donors could find and support any registered US nonprofit with zero platform fees. The user experience was deliberately modeled on consumer social platforms: donor profiles, cause feeds, social sharing, and a clean interface that made discovering and supporting charities feel native to the modern web.

Every.org represented a maturation of the giving platform concept that Helping.org and Network for Good had pioneered years earlier. But where those predecessors felt institutional, Every.org felt contemporary — designed for a generation raised on mobile apps and social feeds. The platform also offered robust API infrastructure, enabling other applications to embed giving functionality. This "giving as infrastructure" approach — treating donation processing as a utility that any application could access — pointed toward a future where charitable giving could be embedded anywhere on the internet, not just on dedicated fundraising pages. Every.org embodied the idea that giving should be as frictionless and ubiquitous as any other online transaction.
