Fundraise Up, founded in 2017, brought rigorous e-commerce conversion optimization to nonprofit donation forms. Their embeddable widget featured AI-powered suggested donation amounts calibrated to each visitor, one-tap payment options (Apple Pay, Google Pay), automatic upsells from one-time to recurring giving, and real-time A/B testing of every element — from button color to ask string to form layout.

The results were dramatic. Organizations switching to Fundraise Up typically saw 2-3x increases in online donation revenue, driven by higher conversion rates and larger average gifts. The platform proved what e-commerce had known for years: that seemingly minor UX decisions — the number of form fields, the position of the submit button, the default gift amount — have enormous impact on completion rates. Fundraise Up also popularized the concept of "intelligent" ask amounts, using donor data and behavioral signals to suggest gift sizes optimized for each individual visitor. The donation form was no longer a static page; it was an adaptive, personalized conversion engine. This shift — treating the donation experience as a product to be optimized — marked a new chapter in fundraising technology.
