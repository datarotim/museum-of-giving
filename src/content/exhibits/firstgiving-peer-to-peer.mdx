FirstGiving launched in 2003 and by 2006 had established itself as a pioneering peer-to-peer fundraising platform with its promise that "Doing good just got easier." The platform allowed individuals to create personal fundraising pages on behalf of registered nonprofits, enabling marathon runners, birthday celebrants, and community organizers to raise money from their personal networks. It was among the first platforms to recognize that donors trust people more than institutions, and that giving individuals the tools to fundraise on behalf of causes would unlock a new channel of giving.

The peer-to-peer model that FirstGiving helped pioneer became the template for an entire category of fundraising technology. By decentralizing the ask — moving it from the organization to the individual supporter — FirstGiving demonstrated that the most effective fundraising often happens between friends, not between an institution and a stranger. The platform processed hundreds of millions of dollars in donations before being acquired by JustGiving in 2013. Its legacy lives on in every charity marathon page, birthday fundraiser, and personal campaign that turns supporters into fundraisers.
