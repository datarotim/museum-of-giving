Macmillan Cancer Support's donation page takes a distinctively British approach to online giving. Rather than defaulting to a single donation form, the page asks "How do you want to donate?" and presents four pathways: single donation, monthly giving, collection (for event-based fundraising), and legacy giving. This multi-pathway design reflects the UK fundraising landscape, where legacy giving — bequests left in wills — represents a significant and growing share of charitable income, particularly for health charities.

The inclusion of legacy giving as a top-level option alongside single and monthly donations is striking. In the US fundraising model, planned giving is typically buried deep in a website's navigation, treated as a specialized product for older, wealthier donors. Macmillan's decision to surface it prominently signals both the cultural normalcy of legacy giving in the UK and the organization's understanding that the donation page is a moment of maximum intent — the right time to plant the seed for every type of giving. The collection option similarly reflects the UK's strong tradition of community and event-based fundraising, from bake sales to sponsored runs. Macmillan's form is a reminder that donation page design is culturally specific, and that the best forms reflect the giving traditions of their audience.
