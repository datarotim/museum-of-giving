The YWCA produced this poster as part of the 1918 United War Work Campaign, a landmark joint fundraising drive that united seven organizations — the YMCA, YWCA, National Catholic War Council, Jewish Welfare Board, War Camp Community Service, American Library Association, and Salvation Army — under a single $170 million goal. The YWCA's contribution focused on recruiting and supporting women volunteers who served overseas as hostess house workers, canteen operators, and recreational leaders for nurses and other women attached to the war effort. "Back Our Girls Over There" deliberately mirrored the language used for male soldiers, asserting that women's service was equally worthy of homefront support.

The United War Work Campaign of November 1918 was a watershed in American fundraising history. It was the first federated campaign of its scale, proving that multiple organizations could coordinate a unified national appeal without cannibalizing each other's donor bases. The campaign raised $203 million in one week — exceeding its goal — and demonstrated the power of coalition fundraising. This model directly influenced the creation of Community Chest drives in the 1920s and ultimately the United Way system. The YWCA's role was also significant for centering women as both the workers being supported and the donors being solicited, expanding the fundraising audience beyond the traditional male breadwinner.
