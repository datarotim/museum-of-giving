Farm Aid was founded in 1985 by Willie Nelson, John Mellencamp, and Neil Young after Bob Dylan remarked at Live Aid that he hoped some of the money raised might help American farmers facing foreclosure. The first Farm Aid concert, held on September 22, 1985, at the Memorial Stadium in Champaign, Illinois, featured 54 acts performing over 14 hours and raised approximately $9 million. The concert was broadcast live to an audience of millions, channeling the Live Aid model toward the American farm crisis.

What set Farm Aid apart was its persistence. Unlike one-off spectacles, Farm Aid became an annual institution — held every year since 1985, it has raised over $80 million for family farmers. The organization didn't just raise money; it built a permanent advocacy infrastructure, operating a hotline for farmers in crisis and lobbying for agricultural policy reform. Farm Aid demonstrated that the benefit concert model could sustain a cause over decades, not just capture a moment. It also proved that domestic causes — less photogenic than famine, less dramatic than disaster — could command celebrity attention and donor dollars when framed with the right narrative and the right artists behind it.
