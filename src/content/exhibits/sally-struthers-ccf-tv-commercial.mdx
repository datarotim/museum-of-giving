Sally Struthers' television appeals for the Christian Children's Fund (later ChildFund International) are among the most iconic and controversial fundraising artifacts of the broadcast era. Beginning in the mid-1980s, Struthers — already famous as Gloria on *All in the Family* — lent her celebrity to long-form direct-response commercials that showed impoverished children in developing countries and asked viewers to "sponsor a child for just pennies a day."

The formula was devastatingly effective: a celebrity spokesperson walking through a village, close-ups of children in distress, a direct emotional appeal, and a phone number displayed continuously. These ads ran during late-night and daytime television, reaching audiences when they were most receptive. The CCF spots pioneered the monthly giving model on television — converting one-time sympathy into recurring $22/month commitments. They became a cultural touchstone, endlessly parodied but rarely matched in fundraising effectiveness, and established the template that organizations like World Vision, Save the Children, and the ASPCA would follow for decades.
