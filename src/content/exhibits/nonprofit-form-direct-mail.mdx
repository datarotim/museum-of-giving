The response device — also called the reply card, donation card, or response form — is the direct mail equivalent of a digital checkout page. It typically includes the donor's name and address (pre-printed for convenience), a set of suggested gift amounts with check boxes, space for a custom amount, and payment options. The best response devices also restate the case for giving in miniature, reinforcing the letter's message at the moment of decision. A pre-addressed, postage-paid return envelope accompanies the card, removing every possible barrier to giving.

Direct mail fundraisers discovered through testing that the response device had an outsized impact on results. The choice of suggested amounts (the "ask string") could shift average gift size by 20% or more. Anchoring with a higher first amount pulled gifts upward; including the donor's previous gift amount as a reference point increased renewal rates. Even the physical design mattered — perforated tear-off cards outperformed loose inserts, and cards that restated the donor's impact ("Yes, I want to provide meals for 50 families") outperformed generic ones. The response device was, in every sense, the original conversion-optimized donation form.
