Camp Hertko Hollow, a camp for children with diabetes operated by the Iowa Diabetes Association, sent this fall appeal letter in 2025 to its donor base. The letter follows the classic direct mail format — a personal story about a camper, a description of the program's impact, and a specific ask tied to the upcoming camp season. For a small, community-based nonprofit, direct mail remains the most reliable way to reach donors who have supported the organization for years or decades.

This appeal is a reminder that direct mail never died — it simply became less visible as digital fundraising captured industry attention and conference agendas. For thousands of small and mid-sized nonprofits across the United States, the annual appeal letter remains the single largest source of individual giving revenue. Camp Hertko Hollow's donor base skews older and local, exactly the demographic that still responds to physical mail at rates that outperform email and social media. The format persists because the fundamentals haven't changed: a real letter, a real story, a real need, and a stamped return envelope.
