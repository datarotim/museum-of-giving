JustGiving launched in the UK in 2000 with a simple but powerful proposition: anyone could create a personal fundraising page linked to a registered charity, share it with friends and family, and collect donations online. By 2003, the platform had established itself as the UK's dominant peer-to-peer fundraising tool, built around sponsored events — marathons, bike rides, head shaves — giving participants a way to replace paper sponsor forms with a digital page.

The impact was transformative. JustGiving democratized fundraising by giving every individual the tools to raise money on behalf of a cause. It shifted the ask from institution-to-donor to friend-to-friend, leveraging social relationships and personal narratives in ways that institutional appeals could not. The platform also introduced transparency features — showing who had donated and how much — that created social proof and competitive dynamics. JustGiving has since processed over GBP 5 billion in donations and inspired dozens of similar platforms worldwide. It proved that the most effective fundraiser isn't always the charity itself — it's the person who cares about the charity.
