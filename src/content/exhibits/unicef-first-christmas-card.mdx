In 1949, UNICEF received a painting from Jitka Samkova, a young girl in Czechoslovakia whose town had received UNICEF aid after the war. The organization reproduced her artwork as a greeting card and sold it to raise funds. The response was immediate and enthusiastic — people wanted to give gifts that also gave back.

The UNICEF Christmas card program grew into one of the most successful cause-related retail operations in history, eventually selling billions of cards worldwide. But its significance extends beyond revenue. It introduced the concept of the "embedded donation" — a purchase that doubles as a charitable act. This idea would eventually evolve into cause marketing, charity products, and the entire social enterprise movement. Every time you buy a product where "proceeds go to," you are living in the world that UNICEF's first Christmas card helped create.
