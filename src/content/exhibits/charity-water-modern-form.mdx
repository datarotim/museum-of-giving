charity: water's 2024 donation form represents the current state of the art in nonprofit fundraising UX. The form is mobile-first, loads instantly, and offers Apple Pay and Google Pay as the primary payment options. Suggested amounts are tied to specific impact metrics ("$40 can give one person clean water"). A recurring gift toggle is prominently featured. The design is minimal, the copy is precise, and every element serves a conversion purpose.

This form is the product of a century of accumulated learning about what makes people give. The emotional imagery traces back to the Red Cross posters of 1917. The specific impact amounts echo CARE's packages from the 1950s. The recurring gift option descends from Greenpeace's canvassing playbook. The conversion optimization reflects lessons from e-commerce and Fundraise Up's A/B testing revolution. The mobile wallet integration embodies the friction-reduction imperative that has driven every technological advance in giving. Stand in front of this exhibit and you see not just a donation form — you see the entire history of fundraising, compressed into a single interface.
