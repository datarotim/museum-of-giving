The Giving Block launched in 2018 to enable nonprofits to accept cryptocurrency donations — Bitcoin, Ethereum, and eventually dozens of other tokens. The platform handled the technical complexity: donors transferred crypto directly, The Giving Block converted it to USD and deposited funds to the charity's account, and both parties received appropriate tax documentation.

The innovation was both technological and financial. Cryptocurrency donations offered significant tax advantages to donors — donating appreciated crypto avoids capital gains tax while providing a full fair-market-value deduction. This created a powerful incentive structure, and the data bore it out: The Giving Block reported that the average crypto donation was approximately $10,000, compared to roughly $120 for the average online cash donation. By making it easy to give crypto, the platform unlocked a new donor demographic — younger, wealthier, tech-native — and a new asset class for philanthropy. The Giving Block demonstrated that fundraising innovation isn't always about asking better; sometimes it's about accepting differently.
