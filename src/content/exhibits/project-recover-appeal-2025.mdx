Project Recover, an organization dedicated to finding and recovering the remains of Americans missing in action from World War II, sent this appeal letter in 2025. The organization uses advanced underwater technology, historical research, and field investigations to locate crash sites and burial locations across the Pacific and Europe. Their direct mail appeals connect donors to specific recovery missions, naming the service members being sought and describing the painstaking search process.

There is a particular resonance between Project Recover's mission and the direct mail format. The organization's work is about memory, obligation, and bringing closure to families who have waited 80 years — themes that carry more weight in a physical letter than in a social media post or email. The donor base includes veterans, military families, and history enthusiasts for whom a letter in the mail feels appropriately solemn. Project Recover's continued use of direct mail in 2025 illustrates a broader truth: the channel endures not just because of donor demographics, but because some messages are better delivered on paper.
