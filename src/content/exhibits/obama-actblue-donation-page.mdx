Barack Obama's 2008 presidential campaign revolutionized political fundraising through an online small-dollar donation strategy built largely on ActBlue's platform. The campaign raised approximately $750 million, with the majority coming from donors giving less than $200. The donation page was optimized for conversion: minimal fields, prominent suggested amounts, one-click recurring options, and aggressive email follow-up sequences.

While technically political rather than charitable, the Obama/ActBlue playbook profoundly influenced nonprofit fundraising. The campaign demonstrated that email-driven, small-dollar, high-volume online fundraising could compete with — and surpass — traditional major donor strategies. ActBlue's technology innovations (saved payment information, one-click giving, A/B-tested donation pages) were quickly adopted by nonprofits. The campaign also proved the power of narrative and identity in fundraising: donors weren't just contributing money, they were joining a movement. Every modern nonprofit that runs email-driven micro-donation campaigns is, in some measure, running a play from the 2008 Obama playbook.
