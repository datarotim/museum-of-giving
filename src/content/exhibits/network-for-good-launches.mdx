Network for Good launched in 2001 as a nonprofit-backed platform designed to make online giving easy, safe, and accessible. Co-founded by AOL, Cisco, and Yahoo, it allowed donors to search for any registered 501(c)(3), make a tax-deductible donation, and receive an instant receipt. For charities without their own online donation capabilities, Network for Good served as a critical intermediary — processing and forwarding gifts on their behalf.

The platform addressed a structural gap in the nonprofit sector: in 2001, the vast majority of charities lacked the technical capacity to accept online donations. Network for Good democratized access to digital fundraising by providing shared infrastructure, much as payment processors like Stripe would later do for e-commerce. The platform eventually processed over $1 billion in donations and later pivoted to providing fundraising software directly to small nonprofits. It represented a pivotal moment in the recognition that online giving required not just willing donors, but an entire ecosystem of technology, trust, and transaction infrastructure.
