MAP International, founded in 1954 to provide medicines and health supplies to communities in need, uses this "Gift Multiplier Voucher" format in its direct mail program. The response device is designed to look like an official voucher, complete with a voucher number and expiration date ("Use by 11/30/25"), creating urgency and a sense of transactional importance. The core psychological lever is the multiplier: each suggested gift amount is paired with its multiplied impact — a $35 gift "can provide $2,275 worth of donated medicine and health supplies." The voucher tells the story of Ali, an 8-month-old from Somalia, putting a face and a name on the need.

The Gift Multiplier Voucher is a sophisticated evolution of the traditional response device. Rather than simply listing suggested gift amounts, it reframes the donor's contribution as an investment with outsized returns. The "every $1 provides $65 worth of donated medicine" claim — grounded in MAP's model of distributing donated pharmaceutical supplies — gives donors a compelling reason to believe their gift goes further here than anywhere else. The inclusion of a QR code, website URL, and credit card option alongside the traditional check-by-mail format reflects modern direct mail's hybrid approach: the physical piece drives the ask, but the donor can complete the transaction through whichever channel they prefer.
