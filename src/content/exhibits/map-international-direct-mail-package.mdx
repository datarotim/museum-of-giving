MAP International, founded in 1954 to provide medicines and health supplies to communities in need, built its donor base through carefully crafted direct mail packages. The typical package included a personalized letter, an outer envelope with compelling teaser copy, and a response device that translated dollar amounts into concrete medical outcomes. The format made global health challenges feel actionable — donors could see exactly what their gift would purchase and where it would go.

The MAP International mail package exemplifies the anatomy of effective nonprofit direct mail: an outer envelope designed to get opened, a letter that tells a story and makes a specific ask, and a reply card that makes giving easy and tangible. Health organizations faced a particular challenge in direct mail — their work was technical and often invisible to donors. By converting supply chain logistics into human stories and specific gift amounts, MAP demonstrated how any mission could be translated into the direct mail format. The response device, with its check boxes and suggested amounts, became a standard component of the direct mail fundraising toolkit.
