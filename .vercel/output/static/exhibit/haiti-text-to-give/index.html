Haiti "Text HAITI to 90999" — $43M via SMS

American Red Cross / mGive · 2010 · US
Mobile

Text HAITI to 90999. Five words, $10, charged to your phone bill. In two weeks, $43 million. Mobile giving had arrived, and the donate button was now in everyone's pocket.

Following the devastating Haiti earthquake of January 12, 2010, the American Red Cross partnered with mobile giving platform mGive to launch a text-to-donate campaign: “Text HAITI to 90999 to donate $10.” The $10 charge appeared on the donor’s phone bill — no credit card needed, no form to fill out. The campaign was promoted by President Obama, broadcast networks, and millions of social media shares. Within two weeks, it raised $43 million from over 3 million donors.

This was mobile giving’s breakout moment. The text-to-give mechanic reduced the donation process to its absolute minimum: five words typed into the device already in your hand. No website, no login, no payment information. The friction was essentially zero. The campaign also demonstrated the explosive amplification potential of social media — each person who texted and then tweeted about it became a fundraiser, reaching their entire network with a simple, shareable call to action. The Haiti campaign proved that the future of impulse giving was mobile, and that reducing friction by even one step could unlock millions in donations that would otherwise never materialize.
