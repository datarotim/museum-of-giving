Classy Peer-to-Peer Campaign

Classy · 2016 · US
Web

Classy turned every supporter into a fundraiser with their own branded page, leaderboard rank, and social sharing tools. Peer-to-peer campaigns scaled the charity walk model to the internet.

By 2016, Classy had refined its peer-to-peer fundraising product into a sophisticated platform that enabled organizations to run large-scale campaigns where individual supporters created personal fundraising pages, set goals, and solicited donations from their networks. The pages were branded to the parent campaign but personalized to each fundraiser, complete with progress thermometers, donor walls, team leaderboards, and social sharing integrations.

Classy’s peer-to-peer tools digitized and scaled a model that had existed in the physical world for decades — the charity walk, the sponsored run, the fundraising gala table captain. But the digital version had advantages the physical one could not match: unlimited geographic reach, real-time progress tracking, automated thank-you communications, and data on every interaction. The leaderboard mechanic — ranking fundraisers by amount raised — introduced gamification and competitive dynamics that drove engagement. Organizations could now run campaigns with thousands of individual fundraisers simultaneously, each working their own network, with the platform handling all the logistics. It was distributed fundraising at scale.
