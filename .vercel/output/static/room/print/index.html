"I Summon You to Comradeship" — Red Cross WWI Poster
American Red Cross 1917

The poster that taught America to give to strangers. Before this, charity was local and personal — the Red Cross made it national, visual, and urgent.

Print
Liberty Bond Newspaper Advertisement
U.S. Treasury Department 1918

The first time a government asked ordinary citizens to fund a war with their wallets instead of their labor. It normalized the idea that giving money was itself a patriotic act.

Print
March of Dimes — FDR Polio Campaign
National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis 1927

The campaign that proved small gifts from millions of people could outperform large gifts from a few. The dime became a unit of democratic philanthropy.

Print
Salvation Army Christmas Appeal Poster
The Salvation Army 1935

The Salvation Army understood something that took the rest of the sector decades to learn: timing is everything. Christmas became the fundraising season because they made it so.

Print
WWII Red Cross War Bond Drive Poster
American Red Cross 1943

By WWII, the Red Cross had perfected the formula: iconic imagery, moral urgency, and a nation already trained to give. The poster was no longer experimental — it was infrastructure.

Print
First UNICEF Christmas Card
UNICEF 1949

A seven-year-old Czech girl painted a thank-you to UNICEF for saving her village. UNICEF turned it into a Christmas card and invented cause-related commerce.

Print
CARE Package Program Mailer
CARE 1958

CARE made the abstract tangible. 'Your $10 sends a package' was the original impact metric — donors could see exactly what their money bought.

Print
UNICEF Trick-or-Treat Collection Box
UNICEF 1962

UNICEF turned Halloween into a fundraising channel by giving children orange boxes and a sense of purpose. It was peer-to-peer fundraising avant la lettre.

Print
Greenpeace Founding Flyer & Canvassing
Greenpeace 1971

Greenpeace proved that confrontation could be a fundraising strategy. The more dramatic the protest, the more effective the appeal that followed.

Print
St. Jude Direct Mail Appeal Letter
St. Jude Children's Research Hospital 1974

St. Jude's direct mail program became the gold standard: a child's photo, a personal story, a specific ask, and a return envelope. The formula hasn't fundamentally changed in 50 years.

Print
Concert for Bangladesh / No Nukes Poster
UNICEF / George Harrison 1979

George Harrison's 1971 Concert for Bangladesh invented the charity concert. By 1979, benefit concerts were a genre. Musicians discovered that conscience and commerce could share a stage.

Live Aid Concert — "Give Us Your Money"
Band Aid Trust 1985

1.9 billion people watched. $127 million raised. Bob Geldof proved that a satellite signal and righteous anger could move more money in a single day than most charities raise in a decade.

Comic Relief / Red Nose Day UK
Comic Relief 1989

Red Nose Day made charity fun. By turning giving into a national party — with a plastic nose as the ticket — Comic Relief raised over GBP 1 billion and proved fundraising didn't have to be solemn.

Salvation Army 100th Anniversary Red Kettle
The Salvation Army 1991

The red kettle has been in continuous operation since 1891 — the longest-running point-of-sale charitable solicitation in history. A hundred years in, the bell still works.
