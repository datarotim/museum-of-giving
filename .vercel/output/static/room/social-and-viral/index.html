Web Social Media
Obama / ActBlue Donation Page
https://www.example.org
Obama for America / ActBlue 2008

Obama's 2008 campaign raised $750 million, much of it in small online donations. ActBlue proved that political fundraising could run on the same rails as charity — and that small donors, aggregated, could outspend billionaires.

American Red Cross / mGive 2010

Text HAITI to 90999. Five words, $10, charged to your phone bill. In two weeks, $43 million. Mobile giving had arrived, and the donate button was now in everyone's pocket.

Mobile
Classy SaaS Donation Form
https://www.example.org
Classy 2011

Classy brought modern product design to nonprofit donation forms. Clean layouts, mobile-responsive, A/B testable. The donation form finally caught up with e-commerce.

Web
GivingTuesday — First Year
https://www.example.org
GivingTuesday (92nd Street Y / UN Foundation) 2012

GivingTuesday created a global day of giving as an antidote to Black Friday consumerism. No organization owns it. No one profits from it. It simply works — $3.1 billion in the US alone by 2023.

Web Social Media
GoFundMe Personal Fundraiser
https://www.example.org
GoFundMe 2013

GoFundMe let anyone become a charity. Medical bills, funerals, emergencies — it raised over $30 billion by turning personal crises into public appeals. It also exposed what a frayed safety net looks like.

Web Social Media
ALS Ice Bucket Challenge — $115M
https://www.example.org
ALS Association 2014

A bucket of ice water, a phone camera, a dare to three friends. The Ice Bucket Challenge raised $115 million in six weeks and proved that virality could be the most powerful fundraising force on earth.

Facebook Birthday Fundraisers
Facebook (Meta) 2015

Facebook turned birthdays into fundraising events. By embedding the ask in a social ritual everyone already performed, they generated over $7 billion in donations — mostly from people who had never fundraised before.
